Adam Coscia

HCC PhD Student @ Georgia Tech

Hey, I'm Adam! I work at the intersection of Visual Analytics and Human-Computer Interaction inventing novel user interaction techniques to investigate how people work with and understand data. I am driven by a desire to answer the question, "Who would use this, and why?"

My current research integrates data augmentation capabilities into the visual analytic process to help users (1) test hypotheses, (2) uncover insights, and (3) build smarter visualizations. I'm also collaborating on developing novel methods for addressing cognitive bias in visual analytics systems.

I'm currently a Human-Centered Computing PhD student at Georgia Tech. I work with Alex Endert as a member of the Visual Analytics Lab. This summer, I am looking forward to working with amazing engineers and scientists at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Caltech, and the ArtCenter College of Design, through their Data to Discovery research initiative.

Outside of my work I enjoy playing and making games, snowboarding, hiking, baseball, music and art. Reach out and say hi!
