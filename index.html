About Me

Hey! I recently joined the HCC PhD program at Georgia Tech in Fall 2020.
I did my undergrad at Stevens Inst of Tech , where I earned a B.S. in Physics and minors in Math and CS. The hard technical skills in Math/CS help me develop practical data-driven systems that bring insight to research teams in academia and industry. Yet, it's the soft human-centered skills I learned from seasoned Physicists —how to think through a problem, collect information, and dissemenate it— that I believe can and should be more accesible to everyone. How? That's what I want to understand: how to make techincal, data-driven discoveries more human-centric.

Research Interests

I've undertaken on-going research in the Visualization Analytics Lab that examines cognitive bias in visual analytics systems, where humans are an integral part of the data visualization process.
One day, I hope to expand my focus to include research in large-scale information displays. I want to help users understand large, multi-faceted, and often complex data, without needing to be a subject-matter expert. In other words... bridging technical prowess and human-centered goals for everyday thinkers and leaders!
