About Me

Hey! I joined the HCC Ph.D. program at GT in Fall 2020. I did my undergrad at Stevens Inst of Tech where I earned a B.S. in Physics and minors in Math and CS.
The hard technical skills in Math/CS help me develop practical data-driven programs that bring insights to research teams in academia and industry.
Yet it's the soft human-centered skills I learned from seasoned Physicists—how to think through a problem, collect information, and dissemenate it— that I believe can and should be more accesible to everyone. How? That's what I hope to research: making techincal, data-driven discoveries more human-centric.

Research Interests

As such, I've undertaken on-going research in the Visualization Analytics Lab that examines cognitive bias in interactive displays, where humans are an integral part of the data visualization process.
One day, I hope to expand my focus to include research in large-scale information displays that allow users to understand large, multi-faceted, and often complex data without needing to be a subject-matter expert—bridging technical prowess and human-centered goals for everyday thinkers and leaders!
