I'm a PhD Candidate in Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Tech, where I work with Alex Endert as a member of the Visual Analytics Lab. My research sits at intersection of visual analytics, data science, human-centered computing, and artificial intelligence. My Ph.D. dissertation was on Detecting and Mitigating Pedagogical Risks in Large Language Models with Visual Analytics.

✨ I'm on the job market for Fall 2026! ✨ I'm seeking either a tenure-track faculty position at a US research university, or a research scientist role at a US national laboratory or FFRDC. Please check out my website below for my latest work and send positions my way that fit me. Thank you!! 💞

I am broadly interested in building tools that help people analyze complex data to make smarter data-driven decisions. As data grows in both volume and complexity, AI systems like large language models (LLMs) are helping industries keep pace by unlocking new analytic and decision-making capabilities. However, relying solely on AI threatens to outsource accountability in critical domains like education, national security, and healthcare, where human oversight and judgement are necessary.

I believe human expertise and adaptability are the keys to making AI safer and more effective. Thus, my research explores how to synergize machine and human intelligence in data analysis. I engage in user-centered co-design with real domain experts to build new visual analytics tools for collaborative sensemaking with AI, as well as evaluating the trustworthiness of AI. I then study how these new tools can enhance user's workflow efficiency while deepening their understanding of complex patterns, making AI more transparent, effective, and usable as an analysis partner.

My research bridges cutting-edge AI with practical, real-world applications, contributing new open-source software, empirical studies, and design guidelines. Over the years, I've collaborated on bridging humans and machines in data analysis with many incredible designers, researchers, engineers, and scientists at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, National Security Agency (NSA), Laboratory for Analytic Sciences (LAS), Adobe Research, NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), Caltech, Vanderbilt, Georgia State, Tufts, Emory, ArtCenter College of Design, and the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute (MBARI).
