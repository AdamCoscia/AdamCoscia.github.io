I'm a PhD Candidate in Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Tech. I work with Alex Endert as a member of the Visual Analytics Lab.

I research and build novel visual analytics interfaces that empower people to discover rich insights in complex data for making smarter, more informed decisions.

My current focus is on leveraging and evaluating AI like large language models (LLMs) in complex workflows. Combining user-centered design with interactive visualizations, I study how visual analytics interfaces can not only make AI more transparent, effective, and usable, but also enhance workflow efficiency and deepen understanding of complex patterns. My research bridges cutting-edge AI with practical, real-world applications, revealing new design opportunities at the intersection of AI, data visualization, and human-computer interaction (HCI).

I've collaborated with incredible designers, researchers, engineers, and scientists at the National Security Agency (NSA), NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), Adobe Research, Caltech, Vanderbilt, Georgia State, Tufts, Emory, ArtCenter College of Design, and the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute (MBARI).
